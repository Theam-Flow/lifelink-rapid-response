# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the **LifeLink Asia** community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on one mission: **saving lives during emergencies**.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

✅ **Life-Saving First**
- Prioritizing features that save lives
- Respecting emergency situations
- Fast response during active disasters
- Clear communication during crises

✅ **Respect & Empathy**
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Showing empathy towards community members
- Focusing on what's best for the community

✅ **Collaboration**
- Supporting other contributors
- Sharing knowledge freely
- Helping newcomers
- Collaborating across organizations

✅ **Professionalism**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being patient with those learning
- Providing constructive feedback
- Acknowledging contributions

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

❌ **Harassment**
- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Personal attacks or insults
- Trolling or inflammatory comments
- Publishing others' private information
- Any conduct which could be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

❌ **Disruption**
- Deliberately disrupting emergency operations
- Spreading misinformation during disasters
- Sabotaging the codebase
- Blocking critical bug fixes

❌ **Discrimination**
- Discriminatory jokes or language
- Exclusion based on any protected characteristic
- Harassment based on identity or background

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Discord server
- Email communications
- Social media accounts
- Community events (online and in-person)
- When representing the project in public

## During Emergencies

### Special Considerations

During active disaster response:

1. **Life-Saving Takes Priority**
   - Critical bugs will be fixed immediately
   - Minor disputes will be postponed
   - All energy focused on emergency response

2. **Patience & Understanding**
   - People may be under extreme stress
   - Response times may be delayed
   - Communication may be brief
   - Forgive mistakes made under pressure

3. **Clear Communication**
   - State urgency level clearly
   - Provide context for emergency requests
   - Follow up when crisis passes

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**conduct@lifelinkasia.org**

For urgent issues during emergencies:
**emergency@lifelinkasia.org**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## During Emergency Situations

### Modified Enforcement for Disasters

During active disaster response (floods, earthquakes, etc.):

1. **Critical Issues Only**
   - Only severe violations will be addressed immediately
   - Minor issues will be documented and addressed after emergency

2. **Focus on Mission**
   - All energy directed toward saving lives
   - Community disputes are temporarily paused
   - Moderation team may be unresponsive

3. **Post-Emergency Review**
   - All incidents will be reviewed after emergency ends
   - Consequences may still apply
   - Lessons learned will be documented

## Our Responsibilities

### For Contributors

**You are expected to:**
- Read and follow this Code of Conduct
- Treat everyone with respect
- Be patient and helpful
- Focus on the mission: saving lives
- Report violations you witness

**You have the right to:**
- Participate freely regardless of background
- Receive respectful treatment
- Work in a harassment-free environment
- Report violations safely
- Receive fair enforcement

### For Maintainers

**We will:**
- Enforce this Code of Conduct fairly
- Protect reporters' privacy
- Respond to reports promptly
- Make enforcement decisions transparently
- Continuously improve our processes

**We will not:**
- Tolerate harassment
- Ignore reports
- Take sides without investigation
- Allow personal biases in enforcement
- Retaliate against reporters
